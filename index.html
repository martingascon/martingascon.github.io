A Brief Bio

A Brief Bio!

Originally from Argentina, I got my PhD in Nuclear Physics at the University of Santiago de Compostela (Spain) in 2010. My PhD thesis was centered around the construction of a gamma-ray and light charged particles detector for the study of nuclear reactions with radioactive beams at relativistic energies.

This all started when I was 14 after reading "A Brief History of Time" by Stephen Hawking. Captivated and enlightened by this book, I started thinking about human beings and how they are able to explain their universe with such a great detail.

I participated in numerous experiments in Nuclear Physics at several accelerator facilities all around Europe and was part of the European project EuroTrans (European program for the transmutation of radioactive waste with particle accelerators). I had a 2 year post-doctoral position at Stanford and my research activities involved studying the non-proportionality response of scintillator materials at low temperatures and high pressures. I was a Physicist postdoctoral fellow at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory developing ceramic nuclear detectors. I have attended more than 20 international conferences and published 40 articles in peer-review journals.

During my PhD, I was awarded a Graduate Fellowship by Ministry of Science and Innovation of Spain. I spent time at several European facilites e.g. Heavy Ions Society (GSI) in Germany, Institut de la Physique Nucleaire (France) and University of Lund in Sweden

Almost two years ago, I transitioned into Data Science. I believe, my background gives me an advantage on this task and find exciting the benefits that can be extracted from the almost unexplored world of big data.
